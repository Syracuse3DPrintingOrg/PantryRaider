# Cooking: the Cook page and the Cook wizard

The Cook page ("What can I cook?") is where Pantry Raider turns what is in your
fridge into something to make tonight. It ranks your own recipes by how much of
each one you already have in stock and floats items expiring soon to the top,
so "this is about to go off" becomes "here is what to make with it". Alongside
your library it pulls ideas from the free web recipe source (TheMealDB, or
Spoonacular if you add a key) in a separate "From the Web" section.

Tapping a recipe, yours or a web one, opens a quick view inside the app with
its photo, ingredients, and steps. Every ingredient you already have is
tagged: an "In stock" chip for things in your inventory and an "On hand" chip
for things on your staples list, leaving a short shopping list of the rest.
From the quick view you can Cook it (make it the current recipe on the On the
Line page), Add to cart (put what you are missing on your shopping list, minus
your staples), Print it, or open the original source.

## The Cook wizard

Not sure what you want yet? The Cook page has a **Launch cook wizard** button
that opens a step-by-step, touch-friendly guide, sized for a kitchen screen.

- **Know what you want?** Type it in and the wizard searches your own recipes
  and the web, or you can ask the AI to write one for you.
- **Not sure?** Tap through a few big-button questions (cuisine, dish type,
  dietary needs) and land on suggestions drawn from your own library and the
  web, with the option to have the AI invent something. Every step is
  skippable, so you can answer as few or as many as you like.

Every result opens the usual quick view, so Cook, Add to cart, and Print all
work from inside the wizard. The AI create option appears only when you have an
AI provider set up.

## Suggested buttons that follow how you cook

The Start Page and Stream Deck editors offer a Suggested section that watches
your real history. Groceries you have bought three or more times in the last
month turn up as ready-made quick-add shopping buttons, and a recipe you cook
often turns up as a Cook shortcut. Each suggestion says why it is offered, adds
with one tap, and can be dismissed for good. Because the suggestions follow
what you actually do, they keep up as your habits change.

## Where recipes live

Recipes, the meal plan, and the shopping list are all built into Pantry Raider,
so the Cook page works with nothing but Pantry Raider and Grocy. If you already
run Mealie you can connect it and keep using it, or copy your library over with
one click. See [Recipes: built in, Mealie optional](recipe-backend-comparison.md)
for the full picture.
